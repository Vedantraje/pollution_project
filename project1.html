Pollution

# Pollution- The presence in or introduction into the environment of a substance which has harmful or poisonous effects.

Types of Pollution -

  1. Air Pollution

    2. Air pollution is the contamination of air due to the presence of substances in the atmosphere that are harmful to the health of humans and other living beings,
    or cause damage to the climate or to materials.

  2. Water Pollution

    3. Water pollution (or aquatic pollution) is the contamination of water bodies, usually as a result of human activities, so that it negatively affects its uses. Water
    bodies include lakes, rivers, oceans, aquifers, reservoirs and groundwater.

  3. Soil Pollution

    4. Soil contamination, soil pollution, or land pollution as a part of land degradation is caused by the presence of xenobiotic (human-made) chemicals or other alteration in
    the natural soil environment. It is typically caused by industrial activity, agricultural chemicals or improper disposal of waste.

  4. Noice Pollution

    5. Noise pollution is considered to be any unwanted or disturbing sound that affects the health and well-being of humans and other organisms. Sound is measured in decibels.

  5. Light pollution

    6. light pollution, unwanted or excessive artificial light. Like noise pollution, light pollution is a form of waste energy that can cause adverse effects and degrade environmental quality.
